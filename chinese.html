Chinese Cuisine

Ingredients:

  • 200g Chow Mein noodles
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1/2 cup mixed vegetables
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 garlic clove (minced)
  • 2 tablespoons chopped spring onions

Instructions:

  1. Boil the Chow Mein noodles as per the package instructions and drain them.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and sauté the garlic for a minute.
  3. Add the mixed vegetables and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes.
  4. Add the boiled noodles, soy sauce, and oyster sauce.
  5. Mix everything well and cook for another 2 minutes.
  6. Garnish with spring onions and serve hot.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Gobi (cauliflower) florets (or 1 cup paneer cubes for variation)
  • 1 tablespoon cornflour
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/2 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying
  • 2 tablespoons oil (for stir-frying)
  • 1 onion (finely chopped)
  • 2-3 green chilies (finely chopped)
  • 1/4 cup bell peppers (finely chopped)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon tomato ketchup
  • 1/2 teaspoon vinegar
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper powder
  • Spring onions for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix cauliflower florets (or paneer cubes) with cornflour, all-purpose flour, ginger-garlic paste, chili powder, and salt.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and deep fry the cauliflower pieces (or paneer cubes) until golden brown. Drain excess oil on paper towels.
  3. In another pan, heat 2 tablespoons oil and sauté chopped onions, green chilies, and bell peppers for 2-3 minutes.
  4. Add soy sauce, tomato ketchup, vinegar, black pepper, and salt. Stir everything well to combine.
  5. Add the fried cauliflower (or paneer) pieces to the sauce and toss gently to coat the florets with the sauce.
  6. Garnish with chopped spring onions and serve hot as a dry snack or starter.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup basmati rice (cooked)
  • 1/2 cup mixed vegetables (carrot, peas, beans)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 1/4 cup spring onions (chopped)
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic (chopped)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon pepper

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan and sauté chopped garlic and onions for 2 minutes.
  2. Add mixed vegetables and cook for 5-6 minutes until tender.
  3. Add soy sauce and mix well with vegetables.
  4. Add cooked rice and toss well to combine with the vegetable mix.
  5. Season with salt and pepper, and garnish with spring onions.
  6. Serve hot as a side dish or main course.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup puffed rice (kurmura)
  • 1/4 cup chopped onions
  • 1/4 cup chopped tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped bell peppers
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon tomato ketchup
  • 1 teaspoon vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon crispy fried noodles (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine puffed rice, chopped onions, tomatoes, and bell peppers.
  2. Mix soy sauce, ketchup, vinegar, red chili powder, salt, and sugar in a small bowl.
  3. Pour the sauce over the puffed rice and toss to combine.
  4. Garnish with crispy fried noodles and serve immediately for the best crunch.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup basmati rice (cooked)
  • 1/4 cup mixed vegetables (peas, carrots, beans)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon tomato ketchup
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 1 tablespoon garlic (chopped)
  • Spring onions (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan, sauté garlic and onions for 2 minutes.
  2. Add mixed vegetables and sauté for another 3-4 minutes.
  3. Add soy sauce, tomato ketchup, and red chili sauce. Stir well.
  4. Mix in the cooked rice and turmeric powder. Stir to combine all ingredients.
  5. Garnish with spring onions and serve hot.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1/2 onion (chopped)
  • 1/4 cup chopped cabbage
  • 1/4 cup chopped carrots
  • 2 tablespoons chopped beans
  • 1/4 cup mushrooms (sliced)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch (mixed with water to make a slurry)
  • 2 cups vegetable broth or water

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan, add onions and sauté for 2-3 minutes.
  2. Add cabbage, carrots, beans, and mushrooms, sauté for 3-4 minutes.
  3. Add soy sauce, vinegar, and vegetable broth. Stir well.
  4. Let it simmer for 10 minutes. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Add the cornstarch slurry to thicken the soup and stir until smooth.
  6. Serve hot, garnished with fried noodles (optional).
