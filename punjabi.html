Punjabi Cuisine

Ingredients:

  • 500g chicken (boneless)
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 tomato (pureed)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 cup cream
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat butter in a pan and sauté the onions until golden brown.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1 minute.
  3. Add pureed tomato, garam masala, and cook for 5 minutes.
  4. Add the chicken pieces and cook until tender.
  5. Add cream and mix well, cook for another 5 minutes.
  6. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve hot with naan or rice.

Ingredients:

  • 250g paneer (cubed)
  • 2 tablespoons yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon oil (for grilling)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, lemon juice, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt.
  2. Marinate the paneer cubes in this mixture for 30 minutes.
  3. Heat oil in a grill pan and cook the marinated paneer cubes for 3-4 minutes on each side.
  4. Serve hot with green chutney and onions.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup whole black lentils (sabut urad dal)
  • 1/4 cup kidney beans (rajma)
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 tomato (pureed)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/4 cup cream

Instructions:

  1. Soak the lentils and kidney beans overnight and cook them in a pressure cooker until soft.
  2. Heat butter in a pan and sauté onions until golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1 minute.
  4. Add pureed tomato, garam masala, and red chili powder. Cook for 5 minutes.
  5. Add the cooked lentils and kidney beans to the pan and simmer for 20 minutes.
  6. Add cream and cook for another 5 minutes. Serve hot with naan or rice.

Ingredients:

  • 250g paneer (sliced)
  • 1/4 cup yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/4 cup cashew nuts (ground)
  • 1/4 cup cream
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt to make a marinade.
  2. Marinate the paneer slices for 30 minutes.
  3. Heat oil in a pan and cook the marinated paneer slices until golden brown on both sides.
  4. In the same pan, add cashew paste and cream, cook for 5 minutes.
  5. Add the cooked paneer slices and simmer for 5 more minutes. Serve hot with naan or rice.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 1 tablespoon oil

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix flour, yeast, sugar, and salt.
  2. Add warm water and knead into a smooth dough. Cover and let it rise for 1-2 hours.
  3. Divide the dough into small balls and roll them into flat discs.
  4. Cook them on a tandoor or in a hot oven until puffed and golden brown.
  5. Brush with ghee or butter and serve hot.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 2 tablespoons ghee
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups water

Instructions:

  1. Heat ghee in a pan, add cumin seeds, and sauté until they splutter.
  2. Add the washed basmati rice and sauté for 2 minutes.
  3. Add water and salt, bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low.
  4. Cover and cook for 10-12 minutes until the rice is done.
  5. Fluff the rice and serve hot with your favorite curry.
